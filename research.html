Bay Area Spanish

My work on Bay Area Spanish focuses on the production and perception of vowels, and how both can be affected by experience with bilingualism.

Bilingualism in Catalonia

My research in bilingualism in Catalonia centers on the production of vowels in Spanish and in Catalan. According to the Speech Learning Model, bilinguals have a shared L1-L2 phonological inventory. When phonological categories are perceived to overlap, they may assimilate to one another, forming a composite category. Conversely, categories may undergo dissimilation, whereby they may be produced in a nonnative-like manner to preserve distinction. I investigate changes in the vowel space over time in order to observe outcomes of language contact and the social and linguistic factors that mediate this change.



Helms, Annie. (2021). Bidirectionality of language contact: Spanish and Catalan vowels. Poster presented virtually at LSA 2021 Annual Meeting. [Poster]

Helms, Annie. (2020). Sociophonetic variability in the production of Spanish /e/ by Catalan-Spanish bilinguals in Barcelona. Talk presented virtually at the 50th Linguistic Symposium on Romance Languages at the University of Texas at Austin. [Slides]
